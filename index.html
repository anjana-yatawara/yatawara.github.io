About

I'm a statistician and applied mathematician studying how financial markets encode and forget information. My research develops new econometric models for volatility persistence, applies machine learning to public health forecasting, and investigates income-based environmental exposure disparities.

I also build AI-powered tools that help students learn statistics — work supported by the California Learning Lab. At CSUB I teach from elementary statistics to mathematical statistics and applied data science, and I mentor undergraduate researchers across multiple funded programs.

▸What I work on

// four domains

Volatility, Memory & Markets

How financial markets remember. New models for volatility persistence across equities, FX, commodities, crypto, and VIX — and the finding that markets forget sub-exponentially.

Building Statistical Thinkers

Teaching statistics with real data, computational tools, and AI-assisted learning — equipping students to think critically with data and work alongside AI, not be replaced by it.

Data Science for Decisions

AI-automated analytics dashboards, time-series forecasting, and rigorous modeling for organizations — academic depth meets applied problem-solving, through CSUB.

KernelStats

A free, open-source statistics platform that runs in your browser — built to replace $1,700–$8,700/yr tools so anyone can run real analysis. No license. No barriers.
